Needfinding

Reporter, tested by Kelvin

Step 1: Observe

Setting the stage: “Kelvin, could you navigate through this app and narrate outloud what your are doing?”

  • Kelvin: “I keep trying to get to the nav menu, but your messenger app notifications keep getting in the way”
  • "I’m going to try and tap on this information — oh — no, I can’t interact with it. [is referring to the visual data of cumulative data on the first four sliding screens]"
  • "If i turn this off, it brings up this thing. what does that mean? [ referring to the awake and asleep toggle on the home screen ] OH, that’s for like NIGHT and DAY. Oh i get that. so i’m assuming that’s like a bedtime thing.”
  • About the data given on the the first report screen - “ I’m pretty sure that 57 DB is NOT quiet. People are talking very loudly right now.”

Some guidance: “Kelvin, could you try and create a log for me?”

  • "I assume that this is what this report button is for."
  • In the ‘what are you doing screen’, he tries to type “working” but the app autocorrects to “workout”
  • “How do I get rid of it? Oh! It’s a toggle… I’m just going to assume that this means off and this means on”
  • In the ‘who are you with’ screen’, he says “These textfields aren’t very responsive” and sees the x on the far right. —> “Oh! — this is what the X is for”

General comments

  • "The main screen doesn’t need to have statistics because most of the time while I’m opening up the app, i’d be there to make a report”. It would be nice to have if I were to show a friend though. It would be right there”
  • “If I were a first time user, I would have no idea what the awake toggle would do”
  • “This reminds me of AprilZero. He does something similar but with things like Fitbit tracking things for him.”

Step 2: Brainstorm User Needs

  1. Needs a way to access the nav when there are other notifications on the phone covering the main navigational elements.
  2. Needs a bigger call to action on the home screen for reporting
  3. Needs a way to specify which elements are interactive and which are not
  4. Needs an explanation for the awake and asleep toggle
  5. Needs a way to delete entries individually without deleting the entire input
  6. Needs a way to account for subjective preference and record it for future use
  7. Needs a way to separate functionality and “coolness”, or separating for the reporting and separating overarching data visuals”
  8. Finding a way to make sure that the user knows about autofill, or the textfields will seem unresponsive for no particular reason.
  9. Needs some sort of explanation element and instructional tour beyond the loading screen explaining what the app is about and what it does.
  10. Needs a reasonable amount of customization, because everyone’s needs are different
  11. Needs something that’s actionable afterwards
